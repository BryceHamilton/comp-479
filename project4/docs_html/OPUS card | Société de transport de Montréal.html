An OPUS card is a smart card on which you can charge all STM transit fares, with the exception of group fares, which are available on tickets only. OPUS cards are used by most transit authorities in the Greater Montréal area, and in the Québec City area.

OPUS card purchase

OPUS card without a photo

Can be loaded with regular fare tickets and passes.

Get your card from station agents or vending machines in métro stations and at other points of sale. When you buy an OPUS card for $6, you must charge it with a fare for at least one trip.
Please note:
- OPUS card is not refundable.
- One user per card (see By-laws A.33.3, r.3.)

Texte important

OPUS cards without a photo are valid for 4 years. Go to carteopus.info to look up your card's expiry date.

Recharging an OPUS card

Texte important

The audio interface for the banking keypad on the reloading terminals (BR) and the fare vending machine (DAT) is now up and running.

You can recharge a valid OPUS card with your choice of transit fares:

  • Online at opusenligne.ca using a computer and OPUS card reader
  • In the métro: at a fare vending machine, a recharging terminal, or a ticket booth Instructions for using the audio interface Accessible Word format
  • At points of sale throughout the city

Registering your OPUS card - Protection against loss or theft

Registering your OPUS card without a photo provides you with a guarantee in the event your card is lost or stolen. You can have it replaced and recoup any transit fares left over when you reported the card missing.

Fare replacement guarantee is automatic for OPUS cards with a photo.

How to register your OPUS card without a photo?

  • In person: go to any station or an Espace client booth
  • Online:  your request will be processed within 10 business days.

Learn more about registered OPUS cards without a photo

How to use the OPUS card

  1. Hold your OPUS card above the fare box on the bus or the card reader on the métro turnstile.
  2. Hold it steady for a few seconds while card is validated.
  3. Validation is confirmed by a green or yellow light and an audio signal (beep).

Lost or stolen card

You must buy a new OPUS card and charge it with a new fare. We encourage you to register your next OPUS card as a guarantee against loss or theft.

Go to any station or an Espace client booth with a photo ID card. You will be given a new OPUS card charged with any fares remaining on the old one when you reported it lost or stolen. You will pay only the cost of a new OPUS card.

Go to the STM photo studio or to an Espace client booth with a photo ID card. You will receive a new OPUS card charged with any fares remaining on the old one when you reported it lost or stolen. You will pay only the cost of a new OPUS card with photo.

Damaged or malfunctioning card

Go to any station or an Espace client booth to replace your OPUS card.

If your card was damaged by misuse, you will have to pay the cost of a new card.
If your card is defective, it will be replaced free of charge.

Be advised that you must pay your fare, as usual, even when going to an Espace client booth to replace a card.

Our advice: Protect your OPUS card by keeping it in a plastic sleeve.

Replacement of an expired OPUS card without a photo

Your OPUS card expires after four (4) years. To find out your card's expiry date, go to carteopus.info

Our advice: Replace your OPUS card without a photo within 90 days of its expiration date.

  • If your valid or expired card is empty of fares or only has a monthly or weekly STM pass: buy a new card.
  • If your valid or expired card has any of these STM fares: single or multiple trips, Unlimited Evening, Unlimited Weekend, 1-day or 3-day pass, take your card and go to any station or an Espace client booth.
  • If your card is still valid and has fares from other transit organizations: take your card and go to an Espace client booth or to your transit provider's service point. If your card has already expired, bring your card and go to your transit organization's customer service centre.
Texte important

Card issuing fees apply when replacing an expired OPUS card without a photo.

Your card was registered?

Be advised that the registered status of your OPUS card is not automatically transferred onto your new card. You must register your new card again.

To do so, please complete the electronic form or go to any station or an Espace client booth.

  • If your valid or expired card is empty of fares or only has a monthly STM pass for current month: go to a métro ticket counter or to an Espace client booth.
  • If your valid or expired card has any of these STM fares: single or multiple trips, Unlimited Evening, Unlimited Weekend, 1-day or 3-day pass, weekly Hebdo pass, take your card and go to a métro ticket counter or to an Espace client booth.
  • If your card is still valid and has fares from other transit organizations: take your card and go to an Espace client booth or at your transit provider's service point. If your card has already expired, bring your card and go to your transit organization's customer service centre.

How to obtain a refund if you already purchased a new card:

Go to an Espace client booth and bring the following with you:

  • Your original purchase receipt for the new OPUS card (it will be returned to you)
  • Your expired OPUS card (or about to expire in less than 90 days)
  • Your new OPUS card

As the registered status of your card is not automatically transferred onto the new card, take this opportunity to register your new OPUS card. 

  • Fill out the form by providing all required information.
  • Send in:
    • your expired OPUS card or the one that expires in less than 90 days
    • the original purchase receipt for the new OPUS card (If you purchased a monthly CAM pass, an equivalent official receipt will be forwarded to you.)
    • the duly completed form.
  • Forward all documents to this address:

    STM
    PO Box 248
    Succursale Place D’Armes
    Montréal, Québec, H2Y 3G7

How you will be refunded:

  • The $6.00 purchase price of the new card will be refunded in the form of: a two-trip fare, valid within the bus and métro system, loaded onto an occasional smart card.
  • Remaining fares on your expired card will be loaded onto an occasional smart card.
  • Please note that postage fees will not be refunded.

Recycling an expired OPUS card

You can now recycle your expired OPUS card by placing it in one of the four Electrobac bins located on our métro network in the following stations:

  • Berri-UQAM
  • Jean-Talon
  • Lionel-Groulx
  • Snowdon

You can also use this bin to recycle your small electronics in a safe and environmentally friendly way.

For more information about Electrobac

The data is permanently and irrevocably deleted and destroyed.

They are taken to recycling centres approved under the Canadian standard of the Recycler Qualification Office to be recycled in Canada. Then they are shredded and sorted. The metals contained in the chip are sent to smelters to have the polyvinyl chloride (PVC) extracted, which is then ground up and sold in the industrial sector.

Tips for your OPUS card

Your OPUS card without photo expires after four years. Don’t let time run out on you! To learn when your card expires and how to replace it, go to carteopus.info. Be advised that issuing a new card is subject to a $6 fee.

For OPUS cards with a photo, validity period appears on back of card. Read more about card fees.

Subscribe to OPUS à l’année and receive your OPUS card at home, pre-loaded with monthly fares. Each month, you paid for it through bank debit or credit card, until such time as you terminate your subscription.

The OPUS & Cie program is for companies that wish to encourage public transit use among their employees. It applies to full fare, annual subscriptions (12 monthly passes), valid on island of Montréal.

With an OPUS card reader, you can buy your transit fares 24 hours a day, seven days a week, right from home. Say goodbye to line-ups!

Avoid waiting in line and buy your monthly pass as of the 20th for the next month.

To ensure your card works optimally when being validated in the bus or métro, it is important it should stay in good condition. Keep it in a rigid plastic case, not in a wallet where, over time, it can wear out or be damaged.
