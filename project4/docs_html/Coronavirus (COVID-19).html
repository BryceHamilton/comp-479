Coronavirus (COVID-19)

Concordia's COVID-19 updates

Concordia continues to monitor COVID-19 response measures and follow the advice of public health agencies. 
Go straight to the return-to-campus plan
Check out our COVID-19 situation dashboard

Key facts & dates  FAQs  Stay informed  COVID-19 research  What to do if you are sick 

Key facts and dates

  • ON CAMPUS: 2 metres of physical distance remain in effect. Concordia's space management guidelines have not changed.
  • Know what to do if you display symptoms, suspect you have COVID-19 or are confirmed positive.
  • The university has launched a COVID-19 situation dashboard, which is updated weekly.
  • The majority of winter 2022 courses will be in person, or a blend of on-campus with online elements. Read more about Concordia’s gradual return to campus.
  • Quebec’s vaccine passport is in effect. It limits access to certain non-essential services to those who can show Quebec-issued proof of COVID-19 vaccination. Learn more about vaccines and the vaccine passport.
  • Procedure masks must be worn when entering university buildings and using shared indoor spaces, including classrooms, libraries, hallways, elevators, lobbies, reception areas, kitchens and washrooms. Learn more about mask requirements.
  • The Webster and Vanier libraries have fully re-opened. Find out more on the Library’s website.
  • Students who need a space on campus for a remote exam can use an appropriate Library study space.

What to do if you are sick, or if you have come into contact with someone who is

If you have COVID-19 symptoms (see Public Health Agency of Canada), please remain at home and call the Quebec helpline: 1-877-644-4545.

The Government of Canada also has an information line: 1-833-784-4387
